2. Describe

[3]

A checksum works at the transport layer of TCP / IP. Describe how a checksum is used to identify packets that have been corrupted during transmission.

Answer: • One for each, to a maximum of 3:
• A checksum formula is applied to the packet before it leaves the source computer
• The source-calculated checksum is added to the packet header
• At the receiving end, the same checksum formula is reapplied
• The new checksum is compared to the received checksum in the packet header
• If the received checksum and the newly calculated checksum do not match, a resend request is issued
