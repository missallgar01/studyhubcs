2. Describe

[3]

A checksum works at the transport layer of TCP / IP. Describe how a checksum is used to identify packets that have been corrupted during transmission.

Answer: One for each, to a maximum of 3:• A checksum formula is applied to the packet before it leaves the source computer (1)• The source-calculated checksum is added to the packet header (1)• At the receiving end, the same checksum formula is reapplied (1)• The new checksum is compared to the received checksum in the packet header (1)• If the received checksum and the newly calculated checksum do not match, a resend request is issued (1)

3. Explain

Explain the reason why at least nine bits are needed to store 300 different binary patterns.

Answer: One from:• 2⁹ gives 512 (1) patterns whereas 2⁸ gives 256 patterns (1)• 2⁸ (1) does not give enough patterns whereas 2⁹ (1) gives more than enough patterns
