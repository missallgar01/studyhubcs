Issues and impact
Some computer systems use algorithms to make decisions that affect people.
Discuss the ethical issues caused by algorithmic bias.
(Total for question = 6 marks)

Command word focus: DISCUSS
  • Explain what algorithmic bias is
  • Discuss multiple ethical issues
  • Include impacts on people/groups
  • Give examples where possible
Mark scheme – ALL acceptable points (indicative content):
  • Algorithms may be trained on biased data
  • Bias can lead to unfair or discriminatory decisions
  • Some groups may be disadvantaged or excluded
  • Lack of transparency makes decisions hard to challenge
  • Bias may reinforce existing social inequalities
  • People may be denied jobs, loans, or services unfairly
  • Decisions are made without human judgement or empathy
Model full-marks answer:

Algorithmic bias occurs when systems are trained using biased data, causing them to make unfair decisions. This can disadvantage certain groups, for example by rejecting job applications or loan requests unfairly. Because algorithms can lack transparency, affected individuals may not understand or challenge decisions, reinforcing existing inequalities.
