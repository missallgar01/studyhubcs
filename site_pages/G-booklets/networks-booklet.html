Q2.
Devices are connected to networks.
(i) Give two reasons for connecting computers in a network. (2)
(ii) Describe one way a local area network (LAN) is different from a wide area network (WAN). (2)
(iii) A network has a speed of 17.08 megabits per second. Identify the equivalent speed in bits per second. (1)
(iv) Define the term ‘latency’. (1)
(Total for question = 6 marks)

Command word focus: GIVE / DESCRIBE / IDENTIFY / DEFINE
  • Give = list valid points
  • Describe = difference + explanation
  • Identify = select the correct value
  • Define = precise meaning
Mark scheme – ALL acceptable answers:
  • (i) Any two from:
    • Share files/data
    • Share applications/software
    • Collaborate
    • Share peripherals (printers, speakers)
    • Share internet connectivity
    • Access files from any computer / hot desk
    • Central control/management (security, updates, backups)
    • Communication (email, video conferencing, online meetings)
  • (ii) LAN vs WAN:
    • LAN covers a small geographical area / WAN covers a large area (2)
    • LAN uses infrastructure owned by one organisation / WAN uses third-party infrastructure (2)
    • LAN connects individual devices / WAN connects LANs (1+1)
  • (iii) B – 17 080 000 (1)
  • (iv)
    • The delay / amount of time between data being sent and being received (1)
Model full-marks answer:

Computers are connected to networks to share files and peripherals. A LAN covers a small geographical area whereas a WAN covers a large geographical area. 17.08 megabits per second is equal to 17,080,000 bits per second. Latency is the delay between data being sent and received.

Q3. Networks
Networks are described in many different ways.
(i) Give the type of network that covers a small geographical area. (1)
(ii) Name the characteristic of a wireless network that is measured in metres. (1)
(iii) Give two disadvantages of a bus network topology. (2)
(Total for question = 4 marks)

Command word focus: GIVE / NAME
  • Short factual answers
  • Two distinct disadvantages needed
Mark scheme – ALL acceptable answers:
  • (i)
    • LAN (1)
    • Local area network (1)
    • Allow WLAN / PAN
  • (ii)
    • Range (1)
  • (iii) Any two from:
    • The whole network stops working if the backbone cable is broken (1)
    • Number of collisions increases as devices increase (1)
    • Greater security risk as all devices see all packets (1)
    • Network stops if terminators fail (1)
    • Only one device can transmit at a time (1)
Model full-marks answer:

A LAN covers a small geographical area. The wireless characteristic measured in metres is range. In a bus topology the whole network fails if the backbone breaks and only one device can transmit at a time.
